Student Organizations

  • American Medical Student Association (AMSA)
    • Our chapter strives to provide our members with useful, timely information and opportunities to plan and lead volunteer activities, to form relationships with professors, medical students and other premedical students, to learn about the dynamic health-care environment, and to have meaningful and rewarding experiences during their premedical years at KSU.
    • Campus Advisor: Army Lester
    • https://clubs.kennesaw.edu/ksuamsa/

  • Beta Beta Beta (TriBeta)
    • Beta Beta Beta (TriBeta) is an honor and professional society for students of the biological sciences.
    • Campus Advisor: Dr. Jerald Hendrix

  • Environmental Alliance
    • The EA communicates and embeds environmental justice at both the interpersonal and internal levels in order to inspire a just, green, and efficient institution for ourselves and our world. We routinely meet to facilitate social, environmental and collaborative events at KSU. So, whether it be ideas or some love, please feel free to contact an EA Coordinator, an EA Officer, or consult the Guest Book and Forums! Green is the new movement at KSU!
    • Campus Advisor:
    • http://www.freewebs.com/ksuea/

  • Kennesaw State Pre-Dental Association
    • Provide members the opportunities to co mingle with Dental professionals through meetings and find out more about our field so as to give our members a true sense of what this career path holds for them. To guide all of our members through the application and DAT testing process effectively. To help find volunteer and shadowing opportunities for the members to attend together throughout the year.
    • Campus Advisor: Dr. Melanie Griffin
    • http://ksupredent.org
    • Facebook: https://www.facebook.com/groups/309418025831026/

  • Kennesaw State Pre-Veterinary Medical Association (PVMA)
    • To inform students of the opportunities in veterinary science. To educate students who are interested animals. We prepare undergraduate students for a career in veterinary medicine.
    • Campus Advisor: Dr. Scott Reese

  • KSU STEM
    • The KSU STEM Scholars program is designed specifically to reinforce and enhance undergraduates pursuing a STEM major by exposing them to a rich curriculum and laboratory exercises in the STEM subjects (science, technology, engineering, and math). In the process, STEM Scholars purport to increase the retention rate of undergraduates pursuing a STEM major while providing a nurturing environment for personal and academic development. KSU STEM Scholars program is composed of academic enrichment, research, and community outreach. STEM Scholars are given various opportunities to enhance their leadership, confidence, professional, and teaching skills in the process.
    • Campus Advisor: Dr. Army Lester
